# Cover Letter For BMC Public Health v1

Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, **“Air pollution-attributable respiratory burden in East Asia: breakpoint trends, four-factor decomposition, and vulnerability profiling from GBD 2021,”** for publication as a Research Article in *BMC Public Health*.

This study evaluates PM2.5-attributable chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), PM2.5-attributable lower respiratory infections (LRI), and ozone-attributable COPD across China, Japan, the Republic of Korea, East Asia, Global, and High SDI from 1990 to 2021. The manuscript is designed to move beyond routine descriptive GBD reporting by combining prespecified breakpoint model comparison, a four-factor accounting decomposition of burden-count change, and age-sex vulnerability profiling.

This submission is not a simple update of our 2024 *BMJ Open* paper on PM2.5-attributable COPD in China, Japan, and South Korea. Compared with that earlier study, the present manuscript adds two new risk-outcome pairs (PM2.5-attributable LRI and ozone-attributable COPD), expands the comparative frame to East Asia, Global, and High SDI, and adds a three-layer interpretation module built around prespecified breakpoint model comparison, four-factor accounting decomposition, and age-sex vulnerability profiling. Its intended contribution is therefore a broader comparative interpretation of respiratory burden transition, not merely a later-year extension of a single prior GBD analysis.

We believe the manuscript fits *BMC Public Health* because it addresses a policy-relevant public-health question at the intersection of environmental exposure, respiratory disease burden, and population aging, while emphasizing transparent and reproducible analysis. The breakpoint module is presented as a constrained descriptive model comparison rather than a comprehensive change-point search, and the four-factor module is positioned as an accounting interpretation layer rather than a mechanistic causal tool. Sensitivity analyses for `1990–2019`, capped versus uncapped implied PAF, annual-chain decomposition, and the common-age-set rerun were included to keep the interpretation conservative.

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by another journal. All authors have approved the submission. The authors declare that they have no competing interests. The study used publicly accessible, de-identified secondary aggregate estimates, so ethics approval was not required, and informed consent and trial registration were not applicable.
