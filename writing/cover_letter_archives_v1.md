# Cover Letter For Archives of Public Health v1

Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, **“Air pollution-attributable respiratory burden in East Asia: breakpoint trends, four-factor decomposition, and vulnerability profiling from GBD 2021,”** for publication as a Research Article in *Archives of Public Health*.

This study examines PM2.5-attributable chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), PM2.5-attributable lower respiratory infections (LRI), and ozone-attributable COPD across China, Japan, the Republic of Korea, East Asia, Global, and High SDI from 1990 to 2021. Rather than presenting a descriptive database summary alone, the manuscript integrates a constrained breakpoint analysis, a four-factor accounting decomposition of burden count change, and age-sex vulnerability profiling to clarify how demographic aging, the underlying total disease burden pool, and attributable-fraction dynamics jointly reshape the interpretation of residual respiratory burden in East Asia.

We believe the manuscript is a strong fit for *Archives of Public Health* for three reasons. First, it addresses a public-health burden question with direct relevance to environmental risk, demographic transition, and vulnerable-population planning. Second, it contributes a methods-aware burden-of-disease application by replacing a single generic rate-change term with a four-factor accounting framework that distinguishes population growth, aging, underlying total disease rate change, and population attributable fraction (PAF) change. Third, it identifies a substantive shift in burden structure, showing that residual respiratory burden in East Asia is increasingly concentrated in older age groups even where age-standardized rates have improved.

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by another journal. All authors have approved the submission. The authors of this submission are **Zihao Liu** (first author) and **Bing Li** (corresponding author). The analysis workflow is reproducible through a version-controlled codebase, structured GBD download manifest, and documented data-access pathway based on the official GBD Results Tool. Raw IHME downloads are not redistributed, but the manuscript includes explicit data-availability and code-availability statements.
