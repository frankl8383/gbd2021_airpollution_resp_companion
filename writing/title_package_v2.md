## Highlights

1. **Under the prespecified comparison between no-breakpoint and one-breakpoint models, the one-break specification was preferred for all analyzed age-standardized series.**
2. **Population aging exerted major upward contributions to COPD burden counts in China and East Asia even where underlying total disease rates declined.**
3. **PM2.5-attributable LRI deaths in East Asia became far less child-dominated than in the global comparator by 2021.**
4. **The age-70-plus group accounted for dominant COPD DALY burden shares across East Asia under both PM2.5 and ozone analyses.**
5. **Net attributable burden change depended on demographic headwinds together with total-rate and PAF dynamics rather than on a single generic rate-improvement channel.**
